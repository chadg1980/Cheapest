About the Cheapest

Hi my name is Chad and I am Cheap. I don't know how I got this way, but here I am. This website is to show off some money saving tips, and how much money you can save in a month, year, decade, career, etc. I am not a financial professional, just a guy that likes to save money. If you need real financial help, you should look elsewhere.

I am not perfect and I do not follow every section every day, but this is about quantifying the every daily habits over a long period of time. Some of the items here are advice I got from other people, that I am passing along.

Brown Bagging

Brown bagging a lunch is something I saw my father do for his entire 30 year career. I see coworkers going out to eat every day while complaining about money issues. Let's take a finacial journey into how much my father saved over 30 years and how much my coworkers are spending (or will spend) in 30 years. The goal is to think small habits today, over the course of a career.

Even going to teryaki with a drink will cost about $10.00 per day. You could spend more, or less if you eat off the dollar menu (But how healthy is that?)

Packing a lunch isn't free , but a sandwhich with lunch meat and yellow mustard will costs an average of $0.75. An average loaf of bread costs $2.40 and can make 10 sandwhiches or $0.24 per day. Lunchmeat can cost $2.50 and can make 5 sandwhiches or $0.50 per day. If you add a side such as a yogurt for $1.00. $1.75 per day for brown bagging per day. If you have expensive taste, it may cost more. When you go out to lunch, you will be paying taxes (almost 10% wher I live) and tip (15%) for a 25% increase in eating out over brown bagging. The totals are based on a 5 day work week.

Go and grab your receipt from your last lunch from work, or look it up on your credit card bill.

Some people spend the money on the drink of choice while, having access to free coffee provided by their workplace! But some still choose to go out and fork over the money anyway!
